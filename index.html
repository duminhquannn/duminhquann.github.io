Unmute

Some minds don’t read in straight lines.

Letters tilt, shift, fall out of mind.

It’s not broken-just different.

This poem isn’t a fix.

It’s a window.

i can feel them sinking into my chest...

my waves...

my ocean...

this pair of eyes, i must hold tightly.
(try moving the eyes in the corner).

They say:

Read

the

word,

child.

But the word..

slips

off

the

tounge

like

butter

off

glass

no grip,

no anchor.

i dig

in desperation

needing a way

to connect...

to feel...

to communicate.

when will I find my answer?

...blurs the world I see,
Lines dissolve unpredictably,

Sharp turns soft

and forms_________break free

Look! They dance

cursive carousel

rotating like dreams i almost remembered but

never quite had.

And they say:

"Just concentrate"

Like I didn't already tie my breath

into knots

trying.

and I am picking up the alphabet

like...

shattered glass on linoleum.

Clocks melt

and numbers twist and slide,

Time plays tricks I cannot guide.

Thirteen flips to thirty-one,

Tomorrow hides behind the sun.

Calendars blur, the months misalign—
I chase the days but lose the line.

1234567890 - but which came first?

left to right or right to left?

I miss

the games

when I was a child

filling blanks with letters.

as I fill my soul with wonder and light that faded when rules replaced play.
